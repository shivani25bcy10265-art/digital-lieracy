# digital-lieracy
This repository contains my Digital Literacy Portfolio assignment completed as part of my role as a Student Digital Ambassador. The project covers five tasks related to presentation tools, professional online profiles, digital platforms, email etiquette, and cybercrime awareness.
Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

I used Canva to create a one-page infographic covering digital literacy definition, safe internet practices, and email etiquette. Icons and visuals represented concepts such as strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, enabling two-factor authentication, and proper email communication. The design aimed to be visually engaging yet informative for fellow students. One interesting part was condensing multiple concepts into a single visual format, balancing clarity with aesthetics. This exercise enhanced my ability to present complex information visually while reinforcing my understanding of safe and responsible digital behavior.
Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio

I created accounts on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Stack overflow. On GitHub, I made a profile README including my name, branch, year, and a personal goal of mastering cloud collaboration and professional networking. 

task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

For coding practice, I used HackerRank and solved the beginner problem “Solve Me First,” which reinforced basic algorithmic thinking and coding logic. Screenshots of the completed challenge and badges were saved. For collaboration, I created a Google Form titled “Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz” with five questions, including multiple-choice and short-answer types. The form link was added to my README, with screenshots of the form and response spreadsheet in task-3-platforms/. These tools help me academically by practicing problem-solving, creating collaborative resources, and preparing for real-world digital tasks.

Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette

I drafted two professional emails: one requesting an assignment extension and another inquiring about a summer internship. Both emails included clear subject lines, formal greetings, structured bodies, and proper sign-offs. A social media checklist was also created with five Do’s and five Don’ts for responsible online communication. This task highlighted the importance of clarity, tone, and structure in digital communication, teaching me how minor lapses can lead to misunderstandings and how to maintain professional interactions online.

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

I focused on phishing for the case study. Phishing involves tricking individuals into revealing sensitive information via fake emails or websites. A scenario depicted a student receiving a fake bank email and entering credentials, leading to potential financial loss and identity theft. The prevention checklist included eight tips such as using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, verifying URLs, and safe practices for online payments. Reporting channels included the National Cyber Crime Portal and helpline 1930. Researching this highlighted the prevalence of cybercrime and reinforced the habit of verifying links and protecting personal information.

Conclusion

This project improved my understanding of digital literacy, professional presence, safe online behavior, coding basics, and cybercrime awareness. It provided hands-on experience with essential digital tools, guided me in creating professional profiles, and enhanced my ability to communicate effectively. Completing all five tasks has prepared me to act as a responsible Student Digital Ambassador and equipped me with skills to navigate and contribute safely in the digital world.
